I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Vector Institute, in Toronto, Ontario. I received my PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at the University of Texas at Austin (UT, Austin) since Fall, 2013. I am advised by Dr. Joydeep Ghosh, director of IDEAL at UT, Austin. I am also affliciated with WNCG. Prior to joining UT, I worked with Amazon Lab126 for two years, prototyping and developing interesting application and platform software using Machine Learning, Image Processing and Computer Vision for next generation consumer products. I completed my Masters from University of California, San Diego, majoring in Computer Engineering in 2011. I completed my undergraduate degree (B. Tech) in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from Visvesvaraya National Institute of Technology, Nagpur, India. For more details, my CV is available here.

Research Interests

My primary research interests are in Machine Learning focusing on building interpretable and explanable models, with applications to (but not limited to) clinical healthcare. I am also interested in problems related to Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency of ML methods.
